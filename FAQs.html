Frequently Asked Questions

What happens after I take my advance?

Once we pay out the advance, it is a condition of our contract, that you will be required to vacate the property. If there are any circumstances out of your control which mean that you cannot vacate on the same day then we may, on occasion, be able to be flexible with timings.

You must use the proceeds of the advance to pay off the existing mortgage on your property. The advance will then be secured against the property by way of a first legal mortgage. Full details and terms are available on request.

Note that once you have drawn down the advance, Vidi will have the right to set the list price for the property, and will have the final say on whether or not to accept an offer on the property. You must also grant Vidi an option to purchase the property for the value of the advance if it gets towards the end of the 12 month advance period without selling.

What does the due diligence process involve and how best can I prepare for the due diligence process?

Our due diligence process involves our legal team reviewing title, property and market information to ensure that the property will be good security for our advance. This is similar to the assessment which would be carried out by any mortgage lender and/or buyer's solicitor.

You can prepare by compiling documents and information that you may have received when you purchased the property, and also in relation to any building work or changes to the property during your ownership.

Can I stay in the property after I take my advance?

Part of the terms of our advance is that you must vacate the property once you receive the money. We can be flexible on the timings of this if there are circumstances out of your control, but we need to ensure that the property can be accessed for viewings at all times. 

Do you need to run a credit check on people in order to release the advance?

We don't run credit checks on home sellers. The advance amount replaces your existing mortgage and it is repaid in full when we sell your home.

We do check charges against your property e.g. how much is still owed on your mortgage as the advance needs to be able to pay off those charges.

Using Vidi doesn't affect your credit rating. 

Do you have any price limitations on the properties you take on?

We can only offer advances on homes valued up to £400,000. 

What happens if my property sells for more than the advance?

You will always receive the full sale price amount minus our fees.

Can I use the advance if I purchased through the Help to Buy scheme?

Given that Help to Buy schemes typically have certain restrictions on repayment and the sale of the property, we currently can not offer the advance on properties purchased through Help to Buy schemes.

Are there any tax or stamp duty implications for me on my next home?

If you take your advance to buy a new home before we sell your home, this means you own two properties at the same time and you would be liable for an additional 3% SDLT (Stamp Duty Land Tax) on the second property.

This is fully refundable upon the sale of your old home.

What can I use my advance for? Do I have to buy another home with it?

If you draw down the advance, you first have to pay off the outstanding balance of your existing mortgage. You can then use the remaining portion of the advance to purchase your new home. At this time the advance can't be used for a purpose other than buying a property.

Who is the legal owner of the property after the advance is drawn?

You remain the legal owner of the property until it is sold. The advance is secured against the property by way of a first charge mortgage.

Are there any conditions to the advance?

Yes, the advance is a mortgage secured against the property and is subject to a full set of terms, conditions and qualification criteria. Full details and terms are available on request.

You must vacate the property when you draw down the advance.

Note that once you have drawn down the advance, Vidi will have the right to set the list price for the property, and will have the final say on whether or not to accept an offer on the property.

You must also grant Vidi an option to purchase the property for a price equal to the amount of the advance if it gets towards the end of the 12 month advance period without selling. Whilst in theory this could result in Vidi purchasing the property for less than the market value, in practice, if the property hasn't sold by the end of the 12 month advance term, it is likely that the advance would be at or above the market value of the property at that time. 

Can I use the advance with a shared ownership property?

Unfortunately, we're unable to offer a advance on a shared ownership property. This is because the housing association which also owns part of the property usually has terms in their contract to be able to offer the property to their own database before it goes on the market openly, and has a part on the final approval on the buyer.

If you have a shared ownership property and you 'staircase' (buy up the remaining portion to own 100% of the property) then you would be able use Vidi.
